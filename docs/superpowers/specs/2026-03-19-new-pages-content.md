## Page 2: How We Train (`/for-families/how-we-train`)

**Purpose:** Explain ADA's training methodology from a family's perspective — "this is why you can trust our doulas."

**Hero:**
- Overline: "FOR FAMILIES"
- Title: "How We Train Our Doulas"
- Subtitle: "A rigorous program that blends traditional Asian postpartum wisdom with modern evidence-based care."

**Section 1:** Training Overview
- 4-5 day intensive program
- Covers: newborn care, postpartum recovery, breastfeeding support, cultural practices (zuo yuezi, etc.), family dynamics
- Available in English, Chinese, Japanese, Korean
- Location: Irvine, CA (7515 Irvine Center Drive)

**Section 2:** What Makes Our Training Different
- **Cultural Integration** — Not just an add-on. Traditional practices (sitting the month, herbal care, dietary customs) are woven into every module.
- **Multilingual Delivery** — Training conducted in the language your doula speaks, not translated after the fact.
- **Practical Assessment** — Every certified doula passes a hands-on evaluation, not just a written test.
- **Insurance Recognition** — Our certification is accepted by major insurance providers.

**Section 3:** Certification Standards
- Written exam: 60 minutes
- Practical evaluation: 30 minutes, one-on-one with examiner
- Certification valid for 3 years
- Continuing education required for renewal

**Section 4:** CTA
- "Find a certified ADA doula" → /for-families/find-a-doula
- "Verify your doula's credentials" → /verify

## Page 3: Find a Doula (`/for-families/find-a-doula`)

**Purpose:** Bridge page that explains the ADA × Cooings relationship and directs families to Cooings for booking.

**Hero:**
- Overline: "FIND A DOULA"
- Title: "Connect with a Certified Doula"
- Subtitle: "ADA partners with professional doula service providers to connect families with certified caregivers."

**Section 1:** How It Works
1. ADA certifies doulas through rigorous training and examination
2. Certified doulas join partner service networks (like Cooings Doula Care)
3. Families connect with doulas through these partners for personalized matching

**Section 2:** Our Partner — Cooings Doula Care
- Brief intro: Cooings is a professional postpartum doula service provider specializing in Asian families
- Services: 26-day live-in postpartum care, lactation support, newborn care
- Coverage areas: LA, Bay Area, San Diego, Seattle, NYC
- Insurance: Medi-Cal, Kaiser, Cigna, Carrot Fertility, Blue Shield
- CTA: "Visit Cooings" (external link to cooings.com) + "Contact ADA" (to /support/contact)

**Section 3:** Other Ways to Find a Doula
- Contact ADA directly: contact@asiandoula.org / (714) 202-6501
- Ask your insurance provider about ADA-certified doulas
- Check with your hospital or birthing center

## Page 5: Scholarship Program (`/programs/scholarship`)

**Purpose:** Describe the scholarship program for aspiring doulas who need financial assistance.

**Hero:**
- Overline: "SCHOLARSHIP"
- Title: "ADA Scholarship Program"
- Subtitle: "Making doula certification accessible to aspiring caregivers from all backgrounds."

**Section 1:** About the Scholarship
- ADA believes financial barriers should not prevent passionate individuals from becoming certified doulas
- Scholarships cover partial or full training and exam fees
- Priority given to candidates from underserved communities, multilingual speakers, and first-generation caregivers

**Section 2:** Eligibility
- Must be enrolled in or accepted to an ADA-approved training program
- Demonstrate financial need
- Commitment to serving Asian communities post-certification
- Multilingual ability preferred but not required

**Section 3:** How to Apply
- Contact ADA at contact@asiandoula.org with:
  - Name and background
  - Which training program you plan to attend
  - Brief statement on why you want to become a doula
  - Any relevant experience
- Applications reviewed on a rolling basis

**Section 4:** CTA
- "Apply Now" → mailto:contact@asiandoula.org?subject=Scholarship%20Application
- "Learn about training" → /become-a-doula/find-a-doula-training
