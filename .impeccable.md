# ADA Website — Design Context

## Users

**Primary: Aspiring Doulas** — Women (often multilingual Asian-Americans) exploring doula certification. They need clear pathways: how to train, how to get certified, what it costs. They're often navigating this alongside a career change and need reassurance that ADA is credible and culturally aligned.

**Secondary: Families** — Expecting or postpartum parents (primarily Asian-American) seeking a certified doula. They need to verify credentials, understand what "culturally integrated care" means, and find/book a doula quickly. Insurance coverage is a top concern.

**Tertiary: Partner Organizations** — Training programs, insurance companies, hospitals evaluating ADA's credibility. They scan for professionalism and institutional legitimacy.

## Brand Personality

**Three words: Warm, Trustworthy, Cultural**

- **Voice**: Confident but never cold. Professional but approachable. Uses "we" language.
- **Tone**: Like a knowledgeable elder sister in the community — credentialed, caring, culturally proud.
- **Emotional goal**: Visitors should feel "these people understand my culture AND meet professional standards."

## Aesthetic Direction (from Framer original site via MCP)

### Visual Tone
- **Light & Airy** — white/cream backgrounds dominate, NOT dark navy heroes on inner pages
- **Warm accents** — 7 pastel Light Shade colors for variety (green, blue, pink, purple, orange, red, yellow)
- **Floating decorative shapes** — geometric shapes (half-circles, stars, hearts, flowers) scattered in sections with rotation and subtle float animation
- **Generous but not excessive spacing** — 80px section padding (`py-20`), 80px gap between homepage sections

### Typography
- **Headings**: DM Serif Display 400 (never bold — the serif weight IS the emphasis)
- **Body**: Outfit 300–600 (light for body text, semibold for labels/nav)
- **Framer heading sizes**: Section title = 36px Medium, Section subtitle = 20px Regular, Card title = 24px Medium, Body = 16px Regular, Small body = 14px Regular
- **Never use**: Inter, Roboto, Arial, Poppins, system fonts on public pages

## Design Principles

1. **Match the Framer original** — The Framer site at asiandoula.org is the primary reference. When in doubt, pull structure from Framer MCP. Exact dimensions, colors, and layout patterns matter.

2. **Warm, not heavy** — Light backgrounds (white, cream, off-white, pastel accents). Dark backgrounds ONLY for homepage hero, testimonials, and CTA sections. Inner page heroes are ALWAYS light.

3. **Floating shapes bring life** — Decorative geometric shapes (half-circles, stars, flowers) with rotation and float animation are a signature element. They make sections feel alive without being distracting.

4. **Information density through variety** — Use different card types, layouts, and section structures to pack in content. The site should feel content-rich like a real non-profit, not sparse like a SaaS landing page.

5. **Dual audience clarity** — Every page should make it immediately clear whether it's for doulas or families.

## Anti-References (What NOT to Do)

- **Dark navy heroes on every page** — Only homepage and CTA sections. Inner pages must be light.
- **Purple gradient stats bands** — Stats should be inline with content, not in flashy separate sections.
- **Single centered text blocks with huge whitespace** — Use multi-column layouts, image grids, card grids.
- **Generic AI aesthetics**: Lucide icon grids, gradient text, glassmorphism, Inter font
- **Sparse/minimal layouts** — This is a content-rich non-profit, not a minimalist portfolio
